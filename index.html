I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at Princeton University, where I am advised by Profs. Alex Glaser and Robert J. Goldston. I am also a affiliate of the Program on Science and Global Security at Princeton, where I conduct research on nuclear nonproliferation and safeguards. My research focuses on leveraging machine learning to enhance the interpretability and trustworthiness of thermofluidic design optimization, with applications in energy systems and nuclear reactor safety. I have also conducted satellite imagery analysis for nuclear forensics, supporting efforts to monitor nuclear proliferation activities in foreign states.

Research

Before 2025, I focused on analyzing visual and thermal satellite imagery of North Korea's Yongbyon Nuclear Research Center. Specifically, I conducted nuclear forensics in estimating fissile material production at the Experimental Light Water Reactor, which is a new facility North Korea began operating in October 2023. This work supported the DOE-NNSA Consortium for Monitoring, Technology, & Verification's Thrust Area 2 in detecting signatures of nuclear proliferation activities in foreign states.
